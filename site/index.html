Introduction to water cooling

Every computer generates heat and that heat needs to be removed. The simplest method is to use air cooling which works perfectly well for many systems. Water cooling, however, uses water to cool computer components.

Why water cool?

Water has over four times the heat capacity of air. Further, because water is denser than air, it has over 3000 times the heat capacity of air per-volume. I.e. to cool something as much as you can with a litre of water, you would need 3000 litres of air. So, air cooling requires a lot of fans moving a lot of air and the faster the fan spins, the louder it gets.

Remember: the harder you push your computer, the hotter it gets. So a better cooling system means you can push your computer harder, for longer.

If you want a computer with maximum performance, that looks good and doesn’t sound like an aircraft taking off, there’s no alternative to water cooling. This comes at a price though. Water cooling is much more expensive and because you periodically have to drain, clean, and refill the loop, it requires more maintenance.

Water Cooling:

  • Pros:
    • Computer runs cooler
      • Components last longer
      • Increased computer performance
    • Quieter
    • Better Asthetics
  • Cons:
    • More expensive
    • Harder to install
    • More maintenance

Air Cooling:

  • Pros:
    • Cheaper
    • Easier to install
    • Less maintenance
  • Cons:
    • Computer runs hotter
      • Components might not last as long
      • Lower computer performance
    • Louder
    • Worse Asthetics
